About Vestura

Clothes that fit the second time, on the first try.

We started Vestura in 2024 because getting dressed online had become a guessing game — one we were tired of losing. This is what we're building instead.

What we're about

Vestura is a wardrobe brand built around certainty — rugby knits, washed caps and raw canvas staples, cut consistently across every drop and shipped in packaging designed to disappear back into the ground, not a landfill.

Side by side

What we found, and what we did about it.

The problem

Online shopping made fit a lottery.

Our solution

Try it on before you even try it on.

Inconsistent sizing between brands, and often between drops from the same brand.
A five-minute Fit Profile that recommends your size before checkout, not after.
Return shipping and repackaging that quietly outweighs the item's own footprint.
Consistent, generously-cut blocks across every colourway and every season.
Plastic mailers and poly bags that outlive the garment they carried.
Seed-lined paper tins that replace the plastic mailer, and grow into a plant pot after.
Our mission

To make getting dressed feel certain again.

We exist to close the gap between what you order and what actually shows up — building garments and a fit system precise enough that "try it on" stops being a leap of faith.

Our vision

A wardrobe industry with nothing wasted, on either end.

Nothing ships that isn't going to be kept and worn, and nothing we send out fails to go back into the ground when its life is done. Fit built in from the start; decay built in from the start.

How we work

Four things we don't compromise on.

01

Fit first

Every pattern starts from a Fit Profile, not a sample size on a runway model.

02

Built to last

Heavyweight cotton, reinforced seams, colours that fade the way denim does — on purpose.

03

Nothing single-use

Tins you plant, tags cut from recycled board, totes meant to replace the bag it came in.

04

Small, deliberate drops

We'd rather sell out of the right sizes than over-produce the wrong ones.
